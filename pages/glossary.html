Aleo

Privacy-focused blockchain platform using zero-knowledge cryptography (ZK-SNARKs) for building private applications. Uses Leo programming language for writing ZK circuits.

Blockchain

Distributed ledger technology that maintains a continuously growing list of records (blocks) linked and secured using cryptography.

Blockchain Interoperability

Ability of different blockchain networks to communicate and share data with each other, enabling cross-chain transactions and applications.

Blockchain Oracle

Service that connects blockchains to external data sources, enabling smart contracts to access real-world information.

CNC Lathe

Computer-controlled machine tool that rotates workpiece for precision turning operations. Capable of complex geometries with tight tolerances.

Consensus Mechanism

Protocol used by blockchain networks to agree on the validity of transactions. Common types include Proof of Work and Proof of Stake.

Cryptocurrency

Digital or virtual currency secured by cryptography, making it nearly impossible to counterfeit. Operates independently of central banks.

Crypto Wallet

Digital tool for storing, sending, and receiving cryptocurrencies. Contains public and private keys for blockchain transactions.

DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)

Organization represented by rules encoded as smart contracts, controlled by members rather than central authority. Enables transparent, democratic governance on the blockchain.

dApp (Decentralized Application)

Application that runs on a decentralized network, typically blockchain, rather than centralized servers. Offers censorship resistance and user control.

DeFi (Decentralized Finance)

Financial services built on blockchain without traditional intermediaries like banks. Includes lending, borrowing, trading, and yield farming protocols.

Ethereum

Decentralized blockchain platform enabling smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps). Uses Ether (ETH) as native cryptocurrency.

Gas Fees

Transaction fees paid to blockchain validators for processing and confirming transactions. Varies based on network congestion and complexity.

Governance Token

Cryptocurrency that gives holders voting rights in decentralized protocols, allowing community-driven decision making and protocol upgrades.

Layer 2 Solutions

Scaling solutions built on top of existing blockchains to increase transaction throughput and reduce fees while maintaining security.

Liquidity Pool

Collection of funds locked in smart contracts to facilitate decentralized trading and lending. Users provide liquidity in exchange for fees.

LLM (Large Language Model)

AI model trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like text. Powers applications like ChatGPT and our Oracle of Fruit.

Mastercam

Industry-leading CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacturing) software for creating CNC toolpaths and programs. Used for mill, lathe, and multi-axis machining.

Mazatrol

Conversational CNC programming language used on Mazak machine tools. Allows intuitive part programming through graphical interface and conversational prompts.

MPC (Multi-Party Computation)

Cryptographic protocol allowing multiple parties to jointly compute a function while keeping inputs private. Used for secure key management and collaborative computation.

Mycelial Networks

Decentralized organizational structures inspired by fungal networks. Emphasizes interconnection, distributed intelligence, and mutual support without hierarchy.

NFT (Non-Fungible Token)

Unique digital asset verified using blockchain technology. Represents ownership of specific items, art, or content that cannot be replicated.

Precision Metrology

Science of measurement ensuring parts meet specified tolerances, often to ¬±0.0001" accuracy. Uses CMMs, micrometers, and optical comparators.

Ritual Intelligence

AI systems designed to support spiritual practices, ceremonies, and contemplative traditions. Combines technology with sacred wisdom for community healing.

Smart Contracts

Self-executing contracts with terms directly written into code. Automatically execute when predetermined conditions are met, eliminating need for intermediaries.

Solidity

Object-oriented programming language for writing smart contracts on Ethereum and EVM-compatible blockchains. Syntax similar to JavaScript and C++.

Soulbound Tokens

Non-transferable NFTs that represent identity, credentials, or achievements tied to a specific wallet address. Cannot be sold or transferred, representing intrinsic value tied to the holder.

Tokenomics

Economic model and incentive structure of a cryptocurrency token, including supply, distribution, utility, and governance mechanisms.

Web3

The next evolution of the internet built on blockchain technology, emphasizing decentralization, user ownership, and peer-to-peer interactions without intermediaries.

Yield Farming

DeFi strategy of lending or staking cryptocurrency to earn rewards, often in the form of additional tokens or interest payments.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZK)

Cryptographic method allowing one party to prove to another that a statement is true without revealing any information beyond the validity of the statement itself. Essential for privacy-preserving blockchain applications.
